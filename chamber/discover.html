Here's what you don't want to miss while in Miami


Bayside Marketplace

Located in the heart of downtown Miami, this open air vendor place is the spot to go for all your shopping



Vizcaya Museum and Garden

This former villa is now the home to an extensive Italian Renaissance Garden. Known for its native woodland landscape, this historic landmark is great for those that love greenery and architecture



Bill Baggs Cape Florida State Park

A piece of living history, the Cape Florida Lighthouse has been standing tall on the shores of Cape Florida for nearly 200 years and is a time capsule containing an immense amount of Florida history



Wynwood Walls

For the artistic eye, this street art museum is the place to be. Full of eclectic restaurants and shops, Wynwood also offers visitors countless opportunities to enjoy the best of international contemporary art, whether in galleries or simply on the street



Pinolandia

As one of Miami's hidden gems, this Nicaraguan restaurant is very popular amongst the locals. Open 24 hours a day, you can always find someone in line waiting for the their food.



Calle Ocho

This street in Miami gives you a deep understanding of the Cuban American relationship and presents each visitor with a magic combination of sound, color, unique flavors and characters in every place you visit. Experience the relationship between music, dance and dominoes and let yourself feel the heart and passion of the people
